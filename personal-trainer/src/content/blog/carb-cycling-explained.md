Carb cycling sounds advanced, but the concept is straightforward: eat more carbohydrates on hard training days and fewer on rest or light days.

On leg day or heavy compound sessions, your muscles are primed to use glycogen — that's when extra rice, oats, or potatoes make sense. On rest days, shift those calories toward protein and vegetables while keeping fats moderate.

You don't need a spreadsheet to start. Train hard four days a week? Eat your biggest carb meals on those four days and keep the other three lighter. Track for two weeks, adjust based on energy and scale trends.
