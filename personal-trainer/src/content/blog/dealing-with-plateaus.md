Every lifter hits a wall eventually. The weights that used to climb every week suddenly won't budge. That's not failure — it's feedback.

First, audit the basics: sleep, protein, stress, and training volume. Often a plateau is a recovery problem disguised as a programming problem. If those are solid, change one variable at a time — add a set, swap an exercise variation, or run a dedicated block for your weakest lift.

Plateaus teach patience. The lifters who break through aren't always the strongest — they're the ones who keep showing up and adjusting instead of quitting.
