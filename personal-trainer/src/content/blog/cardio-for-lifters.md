Lifters often treat cardio like the enemy. Done wrong, it can interfere with strength gains. Done right, it improves recovery, work capacity, and long-term health without killing your progress.

Start with 2–3 sessions per week at low-to-moderate intensity — brisk walking, incline treadmill, or easy cycling. Keep sessions under 30 minutes if you're in a strength-focused block. Save high-intensity intervals for deload weeks or maintenance phases.

The goal isn't to become a marathon runner. It's to build a cardiovascular base that supports harder training and a longer lifting career.
