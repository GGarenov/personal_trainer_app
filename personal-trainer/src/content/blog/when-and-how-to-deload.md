Training hard every week sounds tough, but it's not smart. Your body needs periods of reduced stress to absorb the work you've already done.

A deload typically means cutting volume by 40–50% while keeping intensity moderate. Schedule one every 4–8 weeks depending on training age and stress levels — or when performance stalls and sleep suffers.
